Doctoral Candidate

Alexander J. Davies

Alexander Jake Davies is a doctoral candidate at the London School of Economics and Political Science (LSE), where he is supervised by Professor Peter Trubowitz and Dr. Luca Tardelli. His research centres on international security, foreign policy analysis, and grand strategy, with a particular focus on U.S. politics and the way in which domestic politics shapes foreign policy. He has taught American Grand Strategy and International Security at the LSE, and is the Coordinator for the LSE's Security and Statecraft Research Cluster. Alexander's doctoral thesis, scheduled for submission in 2027, explores the impact of domestic politics on the credibility of alliance commitments.


Outside of Alexander's immediate research interests, he serves as Deputy Editor for Vol. 53 of Millennium: Journal of International Studies. He has served as Research Assistant to Dr. Theresa Squatrito since 2022, and during his MSc, he worked as a Consultant on the LSE's Community Engagement Programme.


Prior to beginning his PhD, Alexander obtained an Distinction in MSc International Relations from the LSE and a First-Class in BA Politics and International Relations from the University of Nottingham, Malaysia.
